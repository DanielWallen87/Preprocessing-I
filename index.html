Work History (and Reasons You Desperately Need Me)

1. Financial Services Representative (Appalachian Community Federal Credit Union)

My #1 responsibility = keeping members with large investment accounts happy. The CEO trusted me to make deals with these members to keep their money locked in. Often, I rewarded them with a slight increase in the deposit rate for expanding their relationship with the credit union. For example: actions such as opening a credit card, setting up a direct deposit, and signing up for online banking. This job taught me how to negotiate effectively.

2. Office Manager (Bristol Family YMCA)

As a liftaholic, I enjoyed combining my interests in fitness and business. The fitness staff often picked my brain for marketing ideas. I also provided new members with tours of the facility and orientations in which I taught them how to use the equipment. My goal was to make a positive first impression so they felt welcome. This position showed me how to teach people below my skill level.

3. Freelance Writer (Many Different Clients, Most of Whom May Not Be Named)

I've written content for several different industries including: automotive, fitness, online marketing, and self-help/personal development. My articles have been read by millions and shared more than 200,000 times. Feel free to browse my archives at Lifehack and Pakwired, as that's where most of the content written in my name lives. These opportunities sharpened my ability to communicate.

4. Waiter (Piccadilly Cafeteria)

I'm aware this might seem out of place. Even so, I loved it. Why? It was an active and engaging job where I met people from a wide variety of backgrounds. Also, one of my biggest mantras is, "The way you do one thing is the way you do everything." If I can't clean a restaurant according to a manager's expectations, then how in the heck would I write code that meets your specifications?
